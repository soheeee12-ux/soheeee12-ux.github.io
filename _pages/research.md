## Ongoing Projects

### Educational Pairing and Life Satisfaction among Marriage Migrant Women in South Korea

This project examines how educational pairings are associated with the life satisfaction of marriage migrant women in South Korea. Using nationally representative data, it examines whether well-being is shaped by educational matching patterns (e.g., homogamy, hypergamy, and hypogamy) or by the absolute educational resources available within couples. Drawing on both educational sorting and migration perspectives, the study explores how educational attainment is linked to well-being in the context of cross-border marriage. The findings shed light on the importance of the availability of absolute educational resources than spouses' relative educational positions.


### Occupational Trajectories and Health Inequality across the Life Course
This project examines how occupational class trajectories across adulthood shape physical and mental health at midlife. Applying sequence analysis, this project identifies seven distinct occupational trajectories between ages 27 and 49, ranging from stable professional careers to persistent labor force detachment. Results show a clear health gradient across trajectories, with disadvantaged pathways associated with poorer physical and mental health at age 50. The strongest health penalties are observed among individuals who spend much of adulthood outside the labor force. Findings highlight the importance of cumulative socioeconomic disadvantage in producing health inequalities over the life course.
